About the Masterclass

Large Language Models (LLMs) are incredibly powerful, but they have limitations: they can hallucinate, and they only know what they were trained on.

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is the industry-standard architecture to solve this. By connecting LLMs to your own custom data, you can build systems that are factual, up-to-date, and deeply tailored to specific domains.

In this masterclass hosted by the Amrita School of AI, we will take you from the fundamental concepts of vectorizing data to advanced architectures like Self RAG and Corrective RAG.

Who is this for?

  • Students and AI enthusiasts eager to build real-world GenAI applications.
  • Software Engineers looking to transition into AI/ML roles.
  • Anyone curious about the mechanics behind state-of-the-art AI chatbots.

What We Will Cover

A comprehensive deep dive into the modern RAG stack, structured for both conceptual understanding and practical application.

1

Intro to RAG

Understanding the architecture, the difference between standard generation and retrieval-augmented generation, and why it's the foundation of modern AI apps.

2

Vector Embeddings & Similarity Scores

Deep dive into how text is converted to numbers. Learn about embedding models, vector databases, and the math behind cosine similarity and distance metrics.

3

Self RAG

Moving beyond basic retrieval. Learn how to train or prompt models to reflect on their own retrieved passages and decide if they are relevant before generating.

4

Corrective RAG (CRAG)

Advanced techniques for robust AI. Discover how to evaluate retrieved documents and implement fallback mechanisms (like web searches) when internal retrieval fails.
